Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan

1936-2021

Pakistan's nuclear program

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan is credited with inventing gas-centrifuge enrichment technology for Pakistan's nuclear program. Additionally, he aided several nations with nuclear technology and information on developing nuclear programs.

Biography

  • Abdul Qadeer Khan, a Pakistani nuclear physicist and metallurgist, is renowned as the "father" of Pakistan's nuclear program.
  • He played a pivotal role in establishing the country's uranium enrichment capabilities and nuclear weapons program.
  • Born in Bhopal, India, in 1936, he later moved to Pakistan and pursued his education in Europe, specializing in metallurgy and gas centrifuges.
  • Khan's work at the Kahuta Research Laboratories led to Pakistan's first successful nuclear tests in 1998, following India's.
